Accenture Social Buzz Presentation

Project Details:

Since being founded in 2010 Social Buzz has seen rapid growth, especially in the past 5 years. They have over 500 million active monthly users and over 100,000 pieces of content daily. The data they collect is highly unstructured and comes in a variety of formats, this makes it difficult to process and analyze. This amount of data is more than they can currently handle. This project explores and visualizes the Social Buzz dataset in order to help them leverage their data and understand how to scale their business properly going forward. ​Data cleaning was conducted in Excel and exploration/visualization in Tableau.

The full interactive Tableau Dashboard can be found Here

This presentation will include an analysis of their data highlighting the top 5 categories with the largest aggregate popularity.​ As well as answering the following questions:
  1. How many individual categories, content types and reaction types are there?
  2. What is the content type breakdown of the top 5 categories?
  3. Which months had the most and least number of posts?
  4. What is the overall sentiment of the posts?

Insights:

  • The top 5 categories make up 36% of the total. Animals is the top category by score.

  • The months of May and January have the most posts. February is the slowest in terms of posts.

  • The overall sentiment across all 16 categories is overwhelmingly Positive at 85% with only 7% Negative reactions

Recommendations:

  • Engagement is lowest between February through April. This could be increased by leveraging the popularity of the top 5 categories.
  • The most popular posts in these categories can be favored over other content and recommended to a wide variety of users during these months which will increase user engagement.
  • To increase sentiment positivity even further, more positive posts should be recommended to users rather than negative posts.
  • There are a lot of reaction types, and this can be confusing for the user as well as creating more complex data. We suggest reducing the amount of reaction types by half.
  • Many of these words are synonyms, one word from each of the synonym groups could be chosen to be used.

About The Project:

This project was part of the Accenture North America Data Analytics and Visualization Virtual Experience Program via Forage. A real life on the job simulation which involved data exploration, cleaning, modelling, analysis, visualization and a powerpoint presentation presented on video.
